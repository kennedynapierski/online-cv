The Artist

"My love for the arts is centered around empathy, inclusion, and building my students to be better humans."

Kennedy Napierski was born in Springfield, Missouri, where she spent many years of her life performing. Today she teaches in her third year at Timpview High School. A graduate of Brigham Young University with a major in Theatre Arts Education, her hope is to create a space where her students feel comfortable and confident while making lifelong friends.

Rehearsal Observations & Preview Questions

  • Is the relationship between the actors clear? Does this read too malicious, or does it strike a relatable chord with the audience?
  • My hope is for the characters to represent Madeline's inner thoughts — is that working? Which moments were strongest, and which appeared muddy?
  • What moments took you out of the world of the show? What dragged, almost to the point of being boring, and how could I fix that?
  • Is it clear that Madeline has grown up over the course of each vignette? If not, what could help?
  • In what moments did the stakes feel heightened? What areas could have been stronger?
  • Do the actors feel like they are performing? What exercises or workshops teach an actor to be authentic?
  • Does adding the mirror sequence confuse the audience about the vignette characters being Madeline's inner thoughts?

Artistic Statement

Why I make theatre

I was never the most talented in the room, the prettiest, or smartest. I was normally in the ensemble playing a part only my parents caught sight of. But being noticed, being the best, was not what gave me worth. Theatre provides confidence.

Theatre is my outlet. My escape from the world. My cove of safety. It is never changing — my rock when things seem too much to bear. It is not only my place, but a place where all feel welcomed and included. I strive for inclusion to be a part of who I am, what I stand for, and how I will amplify it in my classroom. It will be an environment in which judging outward appearances ceases, and investing in relationships to learn and emphasize similarities flourishes. Theatre is for all.

Because of theatre I am better at understanding. I am capable of putting myself in others' shoes. I am a better listener, open to the outpourings of anyone's heart. After the unexpected passing of my father, which happened during the rehearsal period of a high school production, I learned the true intent of others' hearts. Those directors and fellow cast members did not know my struggles, but nevertheless remained by my side. Theatre is an asset of comfort.

I have learned to take risks, and that failure is essential. Choosing to live comfortably is like wanting to dance in the rain, but using an umbrella the entire time. Failure is the training that leads to success. No one, not a single person, will grow if they don't fall — just fall forward. Theatre allows for growth.

I am human, so are you. There is so much unknown in the universe, but what I do know is that theatre changes lives.
